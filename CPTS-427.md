I am an officer in Cybersecurity Group, and I took a turn to present a lesson on Tuesday, March 31st. I chose to talk about Terraform and Ansible. [CSG Meeting slides: Ansible & Terraform](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1WWGwl5ZCkgmv3O2RUbmvVU4xdaGozNwkxSnW41nL-sA/edit?usp=sharing)

## Activity description  
When an officer in CSG make a lesson on a topic of their choice for weekly meetings, we are expected to provide a level of information that freshman or people new to cybersecurity can ingest and take value from. This usually consists of a slide presentation on the topic and its importance in CS/cyber and then a demonstration or hand on lab. For this, I created a PowerPoint on Ansible and Terraform to introduce club members to cloud platform technology.  

## Decisions    
I had to decide what the scope of the hands-on activity would be. Ideally, I would be able to demonstrate both Terraform and Ansible, but many club members come with Windows to club meetings and wihtout a Linux VM. This often makes resolving cross-compatiblity issues the focus of the "lab" section rather than the content itself. I thought that a Terraform example would be much more difficult to make work in this setting, so I decided to create a lab on only Ansible, as this could be easily implemented as a Docker compose project.  

## Contributions  
The Vice President of CSG Justin Van Der Sluys helped out with his stronger background in networking for inspiration on the hands-on lab as well as background information in the slides. My personal contributions were constructing the lab, running the meeting, creating slides on idempotency, Terraform with an example file, and running the hands-on activity.  

## Quality assessment  
Although I tried my best to reduce issues by making the project a Docker compose, Windows users still ran into issues building the image. If I could do this again, I would tell everyone coming to the meeting to come with a Linux VM if they're not running Linux/Mac already. I would rate my performance as highly successful for the learning of club members and my own learning as well. Despite the hiccups, I was told that the presentation and lab were effective and valuble. I also got to work on my presentation and public speaking skills. To improve, I would give myself more time to develop and debug the lab, since there were some bugs that were still not resolved by the time the meeting happened.    

## Career alignment  
This project allowed me to explore my new interest in distributed system/cloud platforms, practice public speaking, and communication to people of varying skill levels.
